# PR0009 – Soft Launch & Distribution

**Status:** Not Started
**Priority:** High
**Dependencies:** MVP complete, PR0008 (UX testing passed)

---

## Objective

Get real users with real money in controlled, low-risk environments to validate product-market fit.

---

## Target Audiences (Phase 1)

### 1. Clubs & Societies
**Why:** Natural fit for group fundraising
**Examples:**
- Sports clubs (team kit fund)
- University societies (event budget)
- Book clubs (guest speaker fund)
- Running clubs (race entry fees)

**Approach:**
- Direct outreach to club secretaries
- Offer free setup support
- Case study opportunity

### 2. Offices & Teams
**Why:** Internal fundraising, social events
**Examples:**
- Office coffee fund
- Team lunch fund
- Charity drive matching
- Secret Santa pool

**Approach:**
- LinkedIn targeting
- Slack community posts
- Office manager outreach

### 3. Friend & Family Networks
**Why:** High trust, forgiving audience
**Examples:**
- Group gifts (birthdays, weddings)
- Holiday funds
- Event tickets (group bookings)

**Approach:**
- Personal network activation
- WhatsApp group sharing
- Referral incentives

## Messaging & Positioning

### Elevator Pitch
"SmashMyBox makes group fundraising simple. Create a box, set a target, and share the link. When it fills, it pays out automatically. No progress bars, just a parcel that gets older each day."

### Key Messages
1. **Simple:** Create in 30 seconds
2. **Transparent:** Fixed targets, no hidden fees
3. **Automatic:** Pays out when full
4. **Visual:** Boxes age, building anticipation
5. **Trustworthy:** Funds secured by Stripe

### Value Propositions by Audience

**Clubs:**
"Stop chasing members for dues. Create a box, share the link, and get paid when it fills."

**Offices:**
"No more awkward cash collections. Digital, automatic, fair."

**Friends:**
"Group gifts made easy. Everyone contributes, no one has to chase."

## Launch Sequence

### Week 1: Soft Soft Launch
- Friends & family only
- 5-10 test boxes
- Real money, small amounts (£10-50)
- Rapid bug fixes

### Week 2-3: Controlled Expansion
- Invite 3-5 clubs/organizations
- Monitor closely
- Collect feedback
- Iterate quickly

### Week 4: Public Soft Launch
- Announce on social media
- Post to communities
- Open registration
- Target: 50 boxes created

### Month 2: Evaluate & Iterate
- Review metrics
- Identify bottlenecks
- Fix critical issues
- Prepare for wider launch

---

## Success Criteria (Month 1)

### Quantitative
- 50 boxes created
- 20 boxes filled
- £5,000 total value processed
- <5% payment failure rate
- >60% creator satisfaction

### Qualitative
- Product-market fit signals
- Positive user feedback
- Low support burden
- Referrals happening organically

---

## Risk Mitigation

### Technical Risks
- Payment failures → Stripe test mode thorough testing
- Server downtime → Monitoring & alerts
- Security breach → Minimal data collection, secure infrastructure

### Business Risks
- No adoption → Pivot messaging or audience
- Legal issues → Clear legal framework (PR0001)
- Fraud → Manual review of suspicious boxes

### Reputational Risks
- Money lost → Funds secured by Stripe (not us)
- Scam accusations → Transparency, clear terms
- Bad press → Responsive communication
